Hi, I'm Christina Xu.

I'm an organizational designer, ethnographer, and teacher based in New York.

I believe in the importance of creating open-ended community spaces, helping people understand each other, and building organizational structures that redistribute power and enable creativity. I'm on a lifelong mission to eat every delicious fruit in the world.

If you're working on something that's a good fit for my values and skills, let's talk!

Recently, I've been:

  • teaching the Orbital 1K course along with Gary Chou and a team of all-star advisors; Gary and I also teach Entrepreneurial Design with Leland Rechis at SVA's Interaction Design MFA program during the spring semester.
  • running interim operations for Everybody at Once, while they bootstrapped from 3 to 9 full-time staff in their first year. Recently, my work has focused on designing new workflows to maximize collaboration, learning, and efficiency for the team.
  • conducting ethnographic research with PL Data, a consultancy that helps organizations integrate quantitative and qualitative research approaches to find useful insights.
  • writing weekly posts at Chrysaora Weekly with the help of supereditor Diana Kimball and more sporadic posts about culture and tech in the Greater China region for 88 Bar.
  • advising for Orbital, a supportive space for independent creators, and for the Data & Society Research Institute.

In my previous lives, I ran Breadpig, a sidekick service for independent creators, where I advised on and produced $2 million worth of crowdfunding campaigns; served as the global coordinator for Awesome Foundation, an international group of guerrilla philanthropists; co-founded ROFLCon, an internet culture conference/convention that ran from 2008-2012; wrote a thesis on the history of interfaces and protocols for instant messaging. It's even nerdier than you imagine.
