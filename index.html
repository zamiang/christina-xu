I believe in the importance of creating community spaces, building subversive infrastructure, nurturing intersectional identities, and extremely loud subwoofers.

Recently, I've been:

  • designing operational processes for Everybody at Once, an audience development consultancy that has grown from 3 to 9 full-time staff in their first year. From my embedded position on the team, I identify communication gaps and processes to streamline, then work with the team to design better ones.
  • working on research projects and training programs with PL Data, a consultancy that helps organizations get more out of their research by integrating quantitative and qualitative sources.
  • writing weekly posts at Chrysaora Weekly with the help of superfriend / supereditor Diana Kimball.
  • teaching Entrepreneurial Design with Gary Chou and Leland Rechis at SVA's Interaction Design MFA program.
  • a member and advisor of Orbital.
  • an advisor for the Data & Society Research Institute.
< p In previous lives, I have helped creators run over $2 million worth of crowdfunding campaigns while running day-to-day operations at Breadpig, served as the global coordinator for Awesome Foundation, co-founded ROFLCon (an internet culture conference/convention), and written a thesis on the history of instant messengers."""

Learning Chinese Thinking Globally

As schools, school districts, colleges, and universities all strive to build strong and sustainable Chinese language programs, it is fundamentally important that they not only prepare students to participate in a global economy where China is a key player, but also make sure the students have the language and cultural skills and the habits of mind necessary to succeed in a highly globalized and interconnected world.
